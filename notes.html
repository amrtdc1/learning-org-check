Opening / Hook (~0:30)

“Why do smart organizations keep making the same mistakes?”

Senge says we suffer from organizational learning disabilities: quick fixes, blame, and not seeing the system we’re part of. The path forward is becoming a Learning Organization that learns faster than the world changes.

Overview: What’s a Learning Organization? (~0:45)

A place where people continually expand their ability to create the results they truly want—by growing, questioning assumptions, sharing a vision, and learning together. Core idea: structure influences behavior. For different results, redesign the system—don’t just fix people.

We’ll cover the five disciplines briefly, then use a 1–5 diagnostic and view our live group snapshot.

The Five Disciplines (~2:30)

1) Personal Mastery

Individuals clarify what truly matters and face current reality honestly. The gap creates creative tension—fuel for learning and improvement.

2) Mental Models

Hidden assumptions shape how we act. We surface, test, and improve them through reflection and inquiry—so decisions get better.

3) Shared Vision

Not a memo—co-created. Turns compliance into commitment. Personal visions feed a genuine collective vision people want to pursue.

4) Team Learning

Dialogue (listening to understand) + Discussion (decide and act). Done well, teams build “operational trust” and think better together than alone. Fosters coordinated, innovative action.

5) Systems Thinking (the Fifth)

The integrator. Helps us see interdependencies, feedback loops, and long-term effects—so we act on root causes, not symptoms. Shifts us from reacting to creating.

Key “Laws” of Systems Thinking (~0:45)

  • Today’s problems come from yesterday’s “solutions.”
  • The harder you push, the harder the system pushes back.
  • Cause & effect aren’t close in time or space.
  • Small changes in the right place can have big impact.
  • There is no blame—we’re all part of the same system.

Use 1 quick example from your org if you have time (e.g., a quick fix that created side effects).

Leadership & Practice (~0:45)

Leaders aren’t just captains steering the ship; they’re designers of the ship. Their real work: craft systems, culture, and simple learning infrastructures so reflection and collaboration happen every day.

  • Integrate learning with daily work
  • Create practice fields for experimentation
  • Build learning communities and infrastructures

Model openness, curiosity, and a bias for seeing structures—not villains.

Takeaway(~0:30)

Learning organizations thrive by:

  • Seeing patterns, not snapshots
  • Encouraging openness and reflection
  • Aligning personal and collective aspirations
  • Fostering teamwork and shared understanding
  • Designing systems for long-term success

Closing (~0:20)

“Learning isn’t a department—it’s our operating system. When we see the system, we stop firefighting and start creating the future we want.”
